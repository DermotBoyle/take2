Discover Madrid

"It takes a lot of imagination to be a good photographer. You need less imagination to be a painter because you can invent things. But in photography everything is so ordinary; it takes a lot of looking before you learn to see the extraordinary." David Bailey

Tourists

Every year Madrid attracts over 6 million tourists to its beautiful streets

Palacio de Cibeles

Originally a post office, this building is now home to Madrid City Concil

Metropolis Building

Inaugurated in 1911 the building was designed by Jules and Raymond Février

Templo De Debod

One of the few works of ancient Egyptian architecture that can be seen outside Egypt and the only one of its kind in Spain.

Oso y el Madroño

It represents the coat of arms of Madrid and is found on the east side of the Puerta del Sol, between Calle de Alcalá and St. Jerónimo run, in the historical centre of the capital.

Plaza Mayor

The foundations of Plaza Mayor were laid, when Philip II's court moved to Madrid, on the site of the former Plaza del Arrabal, where the town's most popular market was located towards the end of the 15th century.

Taxi

If your feet are sore from walking the taxi service of Madrid is the best alternative to viewing the city. Be sure to go soon as the city centre aims to become fully peatonal by 2021!

Malasaña

Malasaña is undoubtedly the hipster neighbourhood of Madrid.

Carrion Building

This iconic, Art Deco landmark built in the 1930s features the name of a soda brand in neon lights.

Royal Palace

The Royal Palace of Madrid is the official residence of the Spanish Royal Family at the city of Madrid, but it is only used for state ceremonies. The palace has 135,000 square metres of floor space and contains 3,418 rooms.
